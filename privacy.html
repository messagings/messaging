Privacy

Your data, explained.

What we collect, why we collect it, and what we don't. No legalese, no surprises.

Right Now — Pre-Launch
Currently, Messaging collects one thing.

Before May 31st, the only data we collect is your email address — and only if you voluntarily join the waitlist. No account is created. No user ID is assigned. No login exists. Your email is used solely to notify you when Messaging launches. The rest of this policy covers how data is handled once the platform is live.

Last updated: May 1, 2026  ·  Effective: May 31, 2026

Your privacy isn't a policy we wrote because we had to. It's the reason Messaging exists. This document explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have. We've written it to actually be readable — not to hide things in legalese.

Account information: When you sign up, we collect your email address and the display name you choose. That's it.

Message metadata: We store timestamps of when messages are sent, and delivery status. We do not store the content of end-to-end encrypted messages — we can't read them.

Device & usage data: We collect basic technical data (device type, OS version, app version) for the purposes of debugging and improving the product. We do not build behavioural profiles from this data.

What we don't collect: We don't collect your contacts list. We don't collect location data. We don't track which links you click inside messages. We don't build advertising profiles.

We use your data for one purpose: to operate Messaging. Specifically:

  • To create and manage your account
  • To deliver your messages reliably
  • To provide customer support when you contact us
  • To send you service-related notifications (security alerts, policy updates)
  • To improve the platform based on aggregated, anonymised usage patterns

We never use your data to show you ads. We never sell, rent, or trade it to third parties. Ever.

We share your data with a small number of trusted service providers who help us operate the platform — cloud infrastructure, email delivery, analytics. These providers are bound by contractual obligations to protect your data and not use it for their own purposes.

We may disclose data if required by valid legal process. When possible, we will notify you before complying. We publish a Transparency Report annually — you can read it here.

Depending on where you're located, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your account and data
  • Object to or restrict certain processing activities
  • Export your data in a portable format

To exercise any of these rights, visit our Data Request page or contact us.

We retain your account data for as long as your account is active. Expiring messages are deleted from our servers according to the timers you set. When you delete your account, your data is permanently removed within 30 days.

We retain certain limited data for longer periods where required by law or for legitimate business purposes (e.g., records of legal requests).

All DMs on Messaging are end-to-end encrypted. Data in transit uses TLS. Data at rest is encrypted. We conduct regular security reviews and promptly address any identified vulnerabilities.

If you discover a security vulnerability, please disclose it responsibly via our contact page.

We use a minimal set of cookies necessary to operate the platform. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. For full details, see our Cookie Policy.
